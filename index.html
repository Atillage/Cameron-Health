Understanding your blood pressure
Blood pressure is written as two numbers: Systolic / Diastolic. Systolic is the pressure when your heart beats; diastolic is the pressure between beats.
Normal
Less than 120/80 mmHg
Keep up the great work!
Elevated
120–129 / less than 80 mmHg
Lifestyle changes recommended.
High — Stage 1
130–139 / 80–89 mmHg
Talk to your doctor. May need medication.
High — Stage 2
140+ / 90+ mmHg
Contact your cardiologist promptly.
⚠ Hypertensive Crisis
Higher than 180/120 mmHg
Seek emergency medical care immediately.
Why reduce cigarettes?
Smoking raises blood pressure immediately and damages artery walls over time. Even cutting back by a few cigarettes a day reduces your cardiac risk. Your cardiologist will be thrilled to see a downward trend in your log.
Steps & heart health
Walking 7,000–10,000 steps daily is associated with lower blood pressure, reduced stress, and better cardiovascular outcomes. Even 5,000 steps is a great starting point if you're just getting active again.
